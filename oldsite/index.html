My name is Sean Flanagan - I'm a concept and technical artist enrolled in the Computational Media program at Georgia Tech. I love playing and making games, and have been doing both for as long as I can remember.

Here's some of my recent work.

 

 

 

 

Created in Unity 3D

 

During my first semester at Georgia Tech, I joined the campus video game development club,
VGDev, where 6 to 7 student-led teams complete a small game each semester. The project I
became a part of, Space Squids, is a fast-paced 2-player racing game akin to Mario Kart.

Except in space. With squids.

Apart from various programming and audio contributions, I was responsible for creating the first
of the game's three levels (which I play in the video), including its constituent models, shaders,
and textures. I also worked with the rest of the team to establish a consistent artstyle that was
both visually appealing and efficient to render.

 

 

Created in GameMaker: Studio

 

I created this lovingly-titled game prototype over the course of four months
during my junior year of high school. It's a 1-to-4-player physics "platformer"
where everyone plays as a softbody blob. Hilarity tends to ensue in groups.

As the single person working on this project, I created all the art and sound assets, and
also programmed an engine within Game Maker which allowed me to build levels with more organic, line-segment-based geometry (instead of the more typical grid/tile-based paradigm.)

I entered this game into the 2014 Georgia Educational Technology Fair
and won first place in the state for 11th/12th-grade Game Design.
